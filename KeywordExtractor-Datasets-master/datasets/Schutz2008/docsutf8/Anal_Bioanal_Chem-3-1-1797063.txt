The effect of the hydrodynamic environment on quorum sensing
Mass transfer is the physical process by which molecules are transported in a system, and it has the potential to affect cell-to-cell signaling in many ways. Delivery of nutrients to the active biomass in the structured community is one example. This could affect the relative metabolic activity of the community, which in turn has the potential to affect signal-production rates. Mass transfer is affected by the hydrodynamics of the bulk fluid and the geometry of the structured community. These two factors affect each other, because a microbial community both shapes, and is shaped by, its external environment.
