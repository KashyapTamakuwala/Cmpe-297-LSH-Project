However, it must be stressed that this is a generalization, and although synchronization at different frequencies may conspire to disturb movement, there may still be subtle differences in the way movement is impaired. This is brought out by the differential effects of low-frequency stimulation on the variation in tapping intervals, evident in differences in the coefficient of variation and hence independent of any differences in tapping rate.
